# IV.E: Personnel roles in simulation

## Domain: Professional Role: Behavior, Capabilities, and Leadership (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Personnel Roles in Simulation:** Understand the boundaries between the 'Operations Specialist,' 'Facilitator,' 'Debriefer,' and 'Embedded Participant' (Confederate). Often, the specialist must play multiple roles; clear transition between these roles is vital.

### [Clinical Application]
Techs acting as confederates (e.g., a family member) provide 'Clinical Realism' that can steer a scenario toward its learning objectives.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Confederate (Embedded Participant):** An individual who plays a role to guide the scenario from the inside.
*   **Operations Specialist:** Manages the tech and environment.
*   **Facilitator:** Manages the overall learning experience.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** The lab is short-staffed, and you are asked to be both the manikin operator and a confederate nurse in the room. What is the risk?
**A:** 'Cognitive Overload' for the operator, leading to delayed manikin responses or missed technical cues, which can break the simulation's fidelity.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Define your role clearly during the pre-brief: 'I will be in the control room as tech support today.'
