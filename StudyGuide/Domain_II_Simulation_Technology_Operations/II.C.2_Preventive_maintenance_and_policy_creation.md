# II.C.2: Preventive maintenance and policy creation

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Preventive/regular maintenance and policy:**
*   **Daily:** Flush fluid lines, power down PCs properly, wipe down manikin skin.
*   **Monthly:** Deep clean fluid tanks (prevent mold), check software updates, inspect cables for fraying.
*   **Annually:** Manufacturer PM (Preventive Maintenance) visits, recalibrate sensors (chest compression depth).

### [Clinical Application]
Failure to flush simulated blood lines leads to mold and clogs, permanently damaging the manikin and ruining future hemorrhage scenarios.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Preventive Maintenance (PM):** Scheduled upkeep to prevent catastrophic failure.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** After a scenario using simulated blood, what is the critical end-of-day maintenance task?
**A:** Flush all fluid lines with a manufacturer-approved cleaning solution (often distilled water and isopropyl alcohol) to prevent blockages.
