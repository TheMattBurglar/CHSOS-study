# V.C.2: Goals, objectives, and outcomes

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Goals, Objectives, and Outcomes:** Support the creation of SMART objectives. The specialist must understand these objectives to ensure the 'Tech' supports them (e.g., if the objective is 'Team Communication', the audio recording is more critical than the manikin's lung sounds).

### [Clinical Application]
Objectives define the 'Success Criteria' for the learner. If the tech fails to capture the data needed to measure these objectives, the simulation is ineffective.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **SMART Objectives:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.
*   **Learning Outcome:** What the learner is expected to know or be able to do at the end of the session.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** An objective is: 'Learners will demonstrate correct CPR technique'. What operational support is required?
**A:** Ensuring the manikin's QCPR sensors are calibrated and the feedback software is running correctly.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Ask yourself: 'What is the one thing the learner MUST do today?' and make sure that tech works 100%.
