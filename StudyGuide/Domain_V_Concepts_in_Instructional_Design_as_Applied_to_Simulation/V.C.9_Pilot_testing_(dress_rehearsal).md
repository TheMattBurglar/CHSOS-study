# V.C.9: Pilot testing (dress rehearsal)

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Pilot Testing (Dress Rehearsal):** Conduct a full 'Run-Through' of new scenarios with 'Beta-Testers' (colleagues or experienced students). This is when technical bugs, logic errors, and missing supplies are identified and fixed.

### [Clinical Application]
Pilot testing ensures that the clinical 'Story' flows logically and that technical failures don't interrupt the learners' experience during the live session.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Pilot Test:** A 'dry run' of the simulation to test all components.
*   **Beta-Tester:** A person who tests the scenario before its official release.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** You are running a pilot and realize the manikin's SpO2 doesn't drop when the 'Airway Obstruction' trigger is activated. What is the fix?
**A:** Adjust the scenario's 'Branching Logic' or 'State Transition' timing in the software to ensure the physiological response matches the clinical event.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Always pilot a new scenario with at least one person who hasn't seen the script before.
