# III.B: Program sustainability and growth (strategic planning)

## Domain: Healthcare Simulation Practices, Principles, and Procedures (25%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Program Sustainability & Growth:** Align equipment purchases with the program's strategic plan and mission. Conduct a 'Gap Analysis' before buying new tech to ensure it meets a specific educational need, not just for the sake of having 'shiny new gear'. Develop redundancy plans for critical equipment.

### [Clinical Application]
Sustainability: Using simulation to improve patient safety (e.g., reducing central line infections) creates a 'Value Proposition' that justifies continued funding and growth.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Gap Analysis:** Identifying the difference between current resources and future needs.
*   **Redundancy Planning:** Having a backup (e.g., a second manikin or task trainer) ready for high-stakes sessions.
*   **ROI (Return on Investment):** Demonstrating value to stakeholders.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** You are asked to recommend a $100k simulator for a lab with a limited budget. What is your first step?
**A:** Perform a needs assessment/gap analysis to determine if the educational objectives can be met with lower-cost alternatives (e.g., task trainers or VR).
