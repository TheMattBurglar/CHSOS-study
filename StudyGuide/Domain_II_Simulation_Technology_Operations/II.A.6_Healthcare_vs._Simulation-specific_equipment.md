# II.A.6: Healthcare vs. Simulation-specific equipment

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Healthcare vs. Simulation Equipment:**
*   **Healthcare Equipment:** Defibrillators, ventilators. *Caution:* Real defibs require specialized simulator adapters to avoid blowing the manikin's motherboard.
*   **Simulation Equipment:** Compressed air compressors, fluid fluid-mixing stations, control PCs.
*   **Safety:** Never mix real meds/fluids with simulated ones. Clearly label "NOT FOR HUMAN USE".

### [Clinical Application]
Using real medical equipment increases physical fidelity but introduces clinical hazards (e.g., accidental shock from a real defib).

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Galvanic Isolation:** Protecting simulator circuits from real electrical shocks.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** A faculty member wants to use a real hospital defibrillator on a high-fidelity manikin. What must the SimOps specialist ensure?
**A:** That the manikin is equipped with energy-absorbing posts/adapters and the software is set to receive live shocks.

*   Audit your lab for clear 'Simulated Use Only' labeling.
