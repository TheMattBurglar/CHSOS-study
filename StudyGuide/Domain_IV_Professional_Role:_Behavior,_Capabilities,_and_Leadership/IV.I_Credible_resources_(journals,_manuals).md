# IV.I: Credible resources (journals, manuals)

## Domain: Professional Role: Behavior, Capabilities, and Leadership (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Credible Resources:** Use peer-reviewed journals (*Simulation in Healthcare*), official manufacturer manuals, and the SSH Standards of Best Practice. Avoid 'Tribal Knowledge' that contradicts evidence-based practice or manufacturer safety guidelines.

### [Clinical Application]
Evidence-based practice in SimOps (e.g., using the correct lubricant) prevents equipment damage and ensures the 'Clinical Truth' of the simulator's behavior.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Peer-Review:** A process where experts evaluate work before it is published.
*   **HSSOBP™ (Healthcare Simulation Standards of Best Practice):** The gold standard for simulation program operations.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** A colleague tells you to use a specific household cleaner on a manikin, but the manual forbids it. What do you do?
**A:** Follow the 'Credible Resource' (the manual) to prevent chemical damage to the manikin skin and inform your colleague of the official guideline.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Keep a physical or digital binder of all current manufacturer manuals in the control room.
