# II.C.1: Technical troubleshooting and corrective action

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Technical troubleshooting and corrective action:**
*   **The Troubleshooting Cycle:** 1. Identify the problem. 2. Establish a theory. 3. Test the theory. 4. Establish a plan of action. 5. Verify system functionality. 6. Document findings.
*   **Common issues:** Power, Network/Connectivity, Software crash, Hardware failure (pneumatics/fluids).

### [Clinical Application]
During an exam, you have roughly 30 seconds to fix an issue before the scenario must be paused, breaking realism.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Root Cause Analysis:** Finding the fundamental reason for a failure, not just treating the symptom.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** The manikin's chest stops rising during a scenario. The software shows respiratory rate is 20. What is the most likely hardware cause?
**A:** The pneumatic air line has disconnected or the internal compressor has failed.
