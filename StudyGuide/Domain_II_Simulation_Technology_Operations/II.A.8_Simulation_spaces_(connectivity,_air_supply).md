# II.A.8: Simulation spaces (connectivity, air supply)

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Simulation Spaces:**
*   **Air Supply:** Compressors provide air for chest rise/fall. They can be noisy and should be isolated from the clinical space if possible. Check PSI requirements.
*   **Connectivity:** Hardwired drops (RJ45) in the ceiling/floor are preferred over Wi-Fi for reliability.
*   **Limitations:** Floor loading weights for heavy equipment (e.g., MRI simulators).

### [Clinical Application]
A loud, knocking air compressor in the room destroys psychological fidelity and distracts from the clinical scenario.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **PSI / Bar:** Pressure measurements for pneumatics.
*   **Acoustic Isolation:** Soundproofing control rooms from sim rooms.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** You are designing a new simulation room. Where should the manikin's air compressor be located?
**A:** In an adjacent, sound-insulated mechanical room or closet to minimize ambient noise.
