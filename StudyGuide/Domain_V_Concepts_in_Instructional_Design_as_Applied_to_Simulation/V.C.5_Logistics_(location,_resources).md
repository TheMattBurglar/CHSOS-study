# V.C.5: Logistics (location, resources)

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Logistics (Location, Resources):** Manage room scheduling, equipment transport, and setup/teardown times. Logistics also include 'In-Situ' simulation (running sim in a real clinical area), which requires coordinating with hospital staff and managing equipment 'footprint'.

### [Clinical Application]
Smooth logistics prevent 'Cognitive Overload' for both learners and faculty, allowing them to stay immersed in the clinical scenario.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **In-Situ Simulation:** Simulation that takes place in the actual clinical environment (e.g., an empty ED room).
*   **Footprint:** The physical space occupied by equipment and personnel.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** You are planning an in-situ simulation in a busy ICU. What is the most critical logistical task?
**A:** Ensuring all simulation equipment (especially 'Simulated Meds') is clearly marked and removed after the session to prevent accidental use on real patients.
