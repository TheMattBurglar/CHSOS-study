# IV.B: Collaboration and teamwork (closed-loop communication)

## Domain: Professional Role: Behavior, Capabilities, and Leadership (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Collaboration and Teamwork:** Use 'Closed-Loop Communication' with faculty during scenarios (e.g., 'Confirming: Manikin heart rate is now 120'). Ensure 'Role Clarity' for all staff (tech, facilitator, actor). Handle technical-clinical conflicts professionally and privately.

### [Clinical Application]
Effective teamwork in the control room prevents 'Role Confusion' that can distract learners and compromise the educational objectives.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Closed-Loop Communication:** Sender states a message -> Receiver repeats it -> Sender confirms.
*   **Role Clarity:** Everyone knows their specific job (e.g., who is the confederate, who is the operator).

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** During a scenario, an instructor asks the manikin to do something technically impossible. How should the operator handle this?
**A:** Use closed-loop communication to suggest a technically feasible alternative that still meets the clinical objective, and debrief with the instructor afterward to manage expectations.
