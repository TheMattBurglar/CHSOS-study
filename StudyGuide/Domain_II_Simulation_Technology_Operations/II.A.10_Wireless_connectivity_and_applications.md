# II.A.10: Wireless connectivity and applications

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Wireless Connectivity:**
*   **Standards:** 802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5) or 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6) are preferred. 5GHz provides faster speed but shorter range; 2.4GHz penetrates walls better.
*   **Interference:** Microwaves, Bluetooth, and adjacent access points can cause dropped packets.
*   **Security:** WPA2-Enterprise (802.1X) requires RADIUS authentication.

### [Clinical Application]
In in-situ simulations (running sim in a real hospital unit), wireless reliability is the biggest failure point due to enterprise network congestion.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **RSSI (Received Signal Strength Indicator):** Measures how strong the Wi-Fi signal is.
*   **Access Point (AP):** Hardware that transmits the Wi-Fi signal.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** A manikin frequently disconnects only when moved to Room B. What is the first troubleshooting step?
**A:** Check the Wi-Fi signal strength (RSSI) and identify potential physical interference (e.g., lead-lined walls) in Room B.
