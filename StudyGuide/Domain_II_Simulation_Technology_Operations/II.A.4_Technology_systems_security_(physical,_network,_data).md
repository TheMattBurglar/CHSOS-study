# II.A.4: Technology systems security (physical, network, data)

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Systems Security:**
*   **Physical:** Server rooms must be locked. Simulators should be secured when not in use.
*   **Network:** WPA2/WPA3 Enterprise for Wi-Fi. Disable unused switch ports.
*   **Data:** FERPA (student records) and HIPAA (if real patient data is used as a template, though it should be de-identified). Video recordings of students are considered educational records.

### [Clinical Application]
If a simulation involves standardized patients (SPs) portraying sensitive scenarios, video recordings must be stored on secure, encrypted servers, not external flash drives.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **FERPA:** Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act.
*   **Encryption:** Data at rest vs. Data in transit.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** A faculty member asks to take a video of a student's poor performance home on a USB drive to grade it. What is the appropriate response?
**A:** Deny the request based on FERPA regulations and provide secure, authenticated access to the video on the simulation network.

*   Review your center's data retention policy for video recordings.
