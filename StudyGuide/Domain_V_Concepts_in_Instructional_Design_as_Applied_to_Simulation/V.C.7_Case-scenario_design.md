# V.C.7: Case/scenario design

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Case/Scenario Design:** Support the 'Storyline' by programming 'States' and 'Transitions' in the software. Create 'Branching Logic' where learner actions (or inactions) lead to different clinical outcomes. Ensure the 'Conceptual Fidelity' makes medical sense.

### [Clinical Application]
Well-designed scenarios allow learners to see the direct consequences of their clinical decisions in a safe environment.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **State:** A specific clinical condition (e.g., 'V-Tach').
*   **Transition:** The trigger that moves the simulator from one state to another (e.g., 'Defibrillation').

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** A scenario is too easy and students finish early. What should the specialist and SME adjust for the next group?
**A:** Add 'Triggers' or 'Distractors' that require higher-level clinical reasoning or faster intervention.
