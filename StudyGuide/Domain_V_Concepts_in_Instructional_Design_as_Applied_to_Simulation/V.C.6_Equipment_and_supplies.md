# V.C.6: Equipment and supplies

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Equipment and Supplies:** Match the 'Tool to the Task'. Ensure all clinical supplies (gauze, meds, tubes) are prepared and consistent with the scenario's fidelity level. Manage the supply chain for consumables to avoid mid-session shortages.

### [Clinical Application]
Under-preparing supplies (e.g., having the wrong size ET tube) breaks the 'Suspension of Disbelief' and distracts from clinical learning.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Consumables:** Items that are used up during a session (tape, IV kits, fake blood).
*   **Fidelity:** The degree of realism of the equipment and environment.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** The objective is 'Chest Tube Insertion'. Which equipment choice is best: A high-fidelity manikin or a dedicated chest-tube task trainer?
**A:** A dedicated task trainer (it allows for repetitive practice and realistic 'feel' that a full-body manikin might not provide for this specific skill).
