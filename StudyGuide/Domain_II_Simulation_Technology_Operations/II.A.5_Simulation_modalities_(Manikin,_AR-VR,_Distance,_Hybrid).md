# II.A.5: Simulation modalities (Manikin, AR/VR, Distance, Hybrid)

## Domain: Simulation Technology Operations (35%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Simulation Modalities:**
*   **Manikins (High/Low Fidelity):** Best for physiological responses and psychomotor skills.
*   **AR/VR:** High initial setup, excellent for spatial awareness and dangerous environments (e.g., fire response).
*   **Standardized Patients (SPs):** Human actors; unparalleled for communication and empathy training.
*   **Hybrid:** Combining SPs with task trainers (e.g., an SP wearing a suturing pad).

### [Clinical Application]
Choosing the right modality impacts the learner's suspension of disbelief (realism). An IV start is best on a task trainer, but breaking bad news requires an SP.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Fidelity:** The degree of realism (Physical, Conceptual, Psychological).
*   **Haptics:** Tactile feedback in VR/Task trainers.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** The learning objective is to teach residents how to deliver a complex diagnosis to a family. Which modality is most appropriate?
**A:** Standardized Patient.
