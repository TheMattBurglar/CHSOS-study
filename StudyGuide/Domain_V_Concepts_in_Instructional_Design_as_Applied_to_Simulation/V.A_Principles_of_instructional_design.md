# V.A: Principles of instructional design

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Principles of Instructional Design:** Utilize the **ADDIE model**. SimOps specialists are most critical in the 'Develop' (building the scenario) and 'Implement' (running the session) phases. Align technical choices with the pedagogical goals.

### [Clinical Application]
Proper instructional design ensures that the simulation is not just a 'tech demo' but a structured educational event with clear clinical outcomes.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **ADDIE:** Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate.
*   **Best Practice:** A technique or method that is more effective at delivering a particular outcome than other techniques.
*   **SME (Subject Matter Expert):** The individual with substantive expertise in the clinical topic area who serves as a consultant.

### [Exam Scenario]
**[Analysis Level] Q:** During the 'Evaluate' phase of ADDIE, you notice that 80% of learners failed to recognize a simulated MI because the EKG was too blurry on the monitor. Where should the correction occur?
**A:** In the **Development** phase. The technical setup (physical fidelity of the monitor) failed to support the educational objective.
