# III.D: Resource utilization and inventory management

## Domain: Healthcare Simulation Practices, Principles, and Procedures (25%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Resource Utilization & Inventory Management:** Use 'FIFO' (First-In, First-Out) for perishable supplies. Manage the 'Lab Footprint' by organizing storage to minimize setup time. Track consumables (gloves, IV kits) to project future budget needs accurately.

### [Clinical Application]
Using expired medical supplies is acceptable in simulation but they must be clearly labeled 'Not for Human Use' to prevent accidental clinical use if they leave the lab.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **FIFO (First-In, First-Out):** Prevents stock from expiring on the shelf.
*   **Consumables:** Single-use items (gauze, meds, tape) that must be tracked for budget purposes.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** The lab is frequently running out of IV start kits. What is the operational solution?
**A:** Implement an inventory tracking system (e.g., a simple kanban card or spreadsheet) and establish 'Par Levels' for reordering.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Set 'Par Levels' for all high-volume supplies like tape, gloves, and alcohol pads.
