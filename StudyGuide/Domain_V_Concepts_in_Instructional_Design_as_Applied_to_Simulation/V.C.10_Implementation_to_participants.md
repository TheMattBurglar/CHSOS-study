# V.C.10: Implementation to participants

## Domain: Concepts in Instructional Design as Applied to Simulation (15%)

### [Status]: 🟢 Studied

### [Technician Perspective]
**Implementation to Participants:** This is the 'Live Show'. The specialist must stay 'Invisible' but attentive, managing the simulator's responses in real-time based on learner actions. Ensure all A/V and recording systems are capturing the data correctly.

### [Clinical Application]
Smooth implementation allows learners to fully immerse themselves in the 'Clinical Reality' of the session without being distracted by 'Tech Noise'.

### [Key Terminology]
*   **Implementation:** The act of running the simulation for the intended learners.
*   **Simulator Artifact:** An unnatural behavior of the simulator that reminds the learner they are in a simulation.

### [Exam Scenario]
**Q:** During a live session, the manikin's compressor starts making a loud, rhythmic thumping noise. What is the priority?
**A:** If it doesn't interfere with safety, keep running. If it distracts learners, pause the sim, fix the 'Artifact', and resume while maintaining the 'Fiction Contract'.

### [Notes & Lab Application]
*   Practice 'Silent Troubleshooting' where you fix minor issues without announcing them to the room.
